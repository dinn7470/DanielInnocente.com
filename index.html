About

I like UX problems where clarity, trust, and accessibility really matter.

My work sits at the intersection of research, product thinking, and front-end craft. I care about understanding what people are trying to do, where systems make that harder, and how design can make the next step feel obvious.

I am especially interested in healthcare, accessibility, and tools that help people make better decisions during stressful or confusing moments.

How I Design

Research first, then make the interface earn its place.

Research deeply

Use interviews, literature review, and competitive analysis to separate user needs from assumptions.

Design accessibly

Prioritize readability, navigation clarity, privacy, and confidence for users with different needs.

Prototype quickly

Move from sketches to Figma to code so ideas can be tested, refined, and explained clearly.
