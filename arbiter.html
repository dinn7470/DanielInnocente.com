The challenge

AI results are only useful when users know what to do next.

Arbiter scans resumes, estimates career level, and recommends roles. My work focused on making that information feel clearer, more actionable, and easier to trust.

Overview

Arbiter helps users understand where they fit in the job market.

Arbiter is an AI-powered job matching tool that analyzes a resume, identifies a user's likely career level, and recommends jobs that match their background and desired path.

My goal was to understand how college students and working professionals interpret the product's outputs, where the interface felt unclear, and how the experience could better help users move forward in their careers.

Problem

The product had powerful information, but users needed more guidance.

Results felt dense

Resume analysis, level classification, committee debate, and job matches competed for attention, making it harder to understand the main takeaway.

Refinement was not central enough

Users needed a simple way to tell the system what mattered to them, such as location, compensation, experience level, industry, or role type.

Career path guidance needed framing

The tool could identify fit, but users also needed help understanding the next level: what skills to build, what roles to pursue, and why.

Research

I interviewed students and professionals to learn what career clarity actually means.

I spoke with college students and people already in the workforce to understand how they evaluate job fit, interpret AI-generated recommendations, and decide what career steps feel realistic or motivating.

Interview focus

What users need from AI career feedback

Participants helped reveal that career recommendations need context, not just output: users want to know why a role fits, what level they are being placed at, and how to improve their chances.

Before

The current experience showed the system working, but not always what mattered most.

After

The redesign gives users more control before and after the resume scan.

My Figma explorations focused on clearer input paths, stronger filtering, and career-path framing so users could better understand not only which jobs fit, but how to move toward the roles they want.

Solution

I reframed Arbiter around fit, clarity, and forward motion.

Make the AI easier to steer

Add custom filters and plain-language refinement so users can shape recommendations around their real constraints and goals.

Separate signal from system noise

Keep transparency around agent debate, but prioritize the user's final level, role fit, and recommended next steps.

Explain career level in context

Help users understand whether a role is a best fit, stretch goal, or future pathway rather than presenting all results as equivalent.

Connect jobs to career progress

Use outputs to show what skills, experiences, or credentials could move the user toward the roles they want next.

What this shows

I design AI interfaces by asking what users can confidently do with the output.

Arbiter already had a compelling technical premise: agents debate a user's level and find roles that fit. My UX work focused on the human side of that promise: whether users could understand the reasoning, adjust the recommendations, and leave with a clearer career path.

This project reflects how I approach emerging AI products: research the user's decision process, reduce ambiguity in the interface, and turn complex system intelligence into concise, actionable guidance.
